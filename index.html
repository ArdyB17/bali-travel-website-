access_time Last Updated : Monday,13-2-2023

Bedugul is a mountain lake resort area in Bali,Indonesia, located in the centre-north region of the island near Lake Bratan on the road between Denpasar and Singaraja the area covers the villages of Bedugul itself, Candikuning, Pancasari, Pacung and Wanagiri amongst others. Jati Luwih rice terraces, Bedugul, Bali. Bedugul is located in the Tabanan Regency,at 48 kilometres (30 mil) north of the city of Denpasar or 20 kilometres (12 mi) south from Singaraja city.

In the area there are three crater lakes Lake Bratan, Lake Buyan, and Lake Tamblingan. Bedugul area enjoys a mild mountain climate due to its location at an altitude of about 1,500 metres (4,900 ft) above sea level. Major sites in Bedugul are the Pura Ulun Danu Bratan water temple and the Bali Botanic Garden. The Botanic Garden, opened in 1959. With a total area of 157.5 hectares (389 acres), it is the largest botanic garden in Indonesia.

Nusa Penida
person_outline Last Visitor : 2987 People
access_time Last Updated : Sunday,12-2-2023

Nusa Penida is an island located near the southeastern Indonesian island of Bali and a district of Klungkung Regency that includes the neighbouring small island of Nusa Lembongan and twelve even smaller islands. The Badung Strait separates the island and Bali. The interior of Nusa Penida is hilly with a maximum altitude of 524 metres. It is drier than the nearby island of Bali. It is one of the major tourist attractions among the three Nusa islands.

There are thirteen small islands nearby – Nusa Lembongan, Nusa Ceningan and eleven even smaller – which are included within the district (kecamatan). Administratively, the kecamatan of the same name, had a population of 45,110 at the 2010 census, covering 202.8 km2,[1] very little changed from 10 years prior.[2]

Pandawa Beach
person_outline Last Visitor : 3467 People
access_time Last Updated : Saturday,11-2-2023

Pandawa Beach (or Pandava Beach) is located at Badung - South Kuta tourist region and one of the many beaches in Bali. In the past, this beach is well known to be one of the regions secret beaches because of the location behind a mountainous area that secluded its location to the unknown. Pandawa beach is surrounded by two cliffs with one of them, carved into the cliff face the statues of the Pandavas and the Kunti goddess.

The six statues from top to bottom are Kunti, Yudhishthira, Bhima, Arjuna, Nakula, and Sahadeva. The beach is well known as a tourist attraction and its water sport use but it is also known as a seaweed algaculture farm. It has a shallow long beach face that is suitable for farming seaweed by the locals. Water sports are also a favorite pass time for tourist at the beach. Surfing is not ideal at the beach although many other sports are known to be favored like beach volley, jet skiing, and paragliding from Timbis cliff to the beach.[1]

Eka Raya Garden


Eka Karya Botanical Garden or better known as Bedugul Botanical Garden is one of the natural attractions in Bali. Bedugul Botanical Garden is located in Candikuning, Baturiti sub-district, Tabanan regency, Bali province. With an area of about 157.5 hectares, Bedugul Botanical Garden is one of the largest botanical gardens in Indonesia. Visiting this botanical garden, visitors can do activities such as picnicking with family, visiting plant collections, playing scooters, or just taking pictures for social media.

Surfing In The Beach


Another attraction of Pandawa Beach is the waves, which are ideal for surfing. The high and consistent waves are a favorite among foreign surfers. Moreover, the beach is not too crowded so surfers can challenge the waves more freely. Even so, the condition of the waves always varies every time. This beach will be visited by professional surfers during the rainy season, which is around October to April. This is the best time for surfers to conquer big waves. But for beginners, try to come between March and November. During these months, the waves tend to be more friendly, making it suitable for learning surfing.
